<p><p>The automotive mineral fluid lubricants market encompasses three main types: paraffinic, naphthenic, and aromatic lubricants. Paraffinic lubricants are characterized by their high stability and low volatility, making them ideal for engine oils. Naphthenic lubricants offer excellent solvency and low-temperature fluidity, which are beneficial for applications requiring good performance under pressure. Aromatic lubricants, while less common in automotive uses, provide enhanced viscosity and temperature resistance, suitable for specialty applications. Each type fulfills specific performance criteria tailored to varying automotive needs.</p></p>
